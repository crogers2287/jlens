## M39 terminology correction

The amended M39 protocol is authoritative. Nuisance features may use only the
**verifier family/type assigned before generation**. The stale phrase `verifier
category` is superseded wherever it could include a verdict, confidence,
evidence, failure subtype, answer distance, post-verification class, manual
outcome, or any output-versus-gold quantity.

M39 must also enforce its frozen pre-generation ambiguity/determinacy policy.
Ambiguity inferred from outputs, samples, telemetry, hidden states, verifier
outcomes, or post-generation review is prohibited from the confirmatory feature
pipeline. The task row remains the unit of analysis and source lineage must stay
grouped across every split.

## Future sequential stopping safety gate

`Doomed from the Start: Early Abort of LLM Agent Episodes via a
Recall-Controlled Probe Cascade` (arXiv:2607.06503) reports early failure
prediction from hidden states and calibrates multiple round-level gates to a
global successful-episode survival target. Its evidence is from TextCraft using
Qwen-2.5-7B and Llama-3.2-3B, not Agents-A1. The paper states that code will be
released later. It does not authorize stopping in M38E or M39.

A future early-abort or truncation protocol is permitted only after M39 first
establishes a forward-only predictive increment and a separate preregistration
is committed. That protocol must freeze before held-out evaluation:

1. one complete episode or task trajectory as the statistical unit;
2. exact gate times, feature sets, thresholds, calibration method, intervention
   semantics, and compute accounting;
3. a **global** bound on aborting eventually successful episodes across the
   entire cascade, not independent per-gate recall claims;
4. separate training, threshold-selection/calibration, validation, and locked
   final-test groups with source-lineage grouping;
5. prospective sample-size and correct/incorrect counts sufficient to certify
   the promised global survival or false-abort target;
6. behavior-only, confidence/logprob, length/round, task, latency, and
   pre-generation verifier-family baselines;
7. compute savings reported only when the frozen safety guarantee is satisfied,
   including all probe and verifier overhead;
8. an explicit `underpowered/inconclusive` outcome when the guarantee cannot be
   certified.

AUROC, average recall, per-round accuracy, or retrospective threshold search is
not production evidence. A policy that cannot prove the frozen global
successful-episode survival guarantee must remain observation-only.

## Semantic workspace localization remains separate

`SpanUQ` (arXiv:2607.05721) reports span-level hidden-state uncertainty probing,
including experiments on Qwen3-30B-A3B. It is a useful future comparator for
claim/span localization, not evidence of Agents-A1 error prediction, causal
semantics, stopping, or correction.

Do not add SpanUQ-style labels or a trainable span decoder to M39. Any future
semantic-workspace localization study requires a separate preregistration,
independent span/claim labels, source-lineage splits, external verification,
calibration, privacy review, and task-level aggregation. Tokens, spans, layers,
and claims from one response may not be treated as independent samples.

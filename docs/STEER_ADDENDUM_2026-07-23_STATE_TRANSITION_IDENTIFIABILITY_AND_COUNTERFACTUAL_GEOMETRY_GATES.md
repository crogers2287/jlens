## New primary evidence and narrow interpretation

Buehler, “Reading and Steering Representations of Materials-Science Mechanisms
in an Open-Weight Language Model,” arXiv `2607.20058v1`, submitted 2026-07-22,
studies direct and Jacobian vocabulary readouts in the dense
`google/gemma-4-e4b-it` checkpoint. The reported model has 42 decoder layers and
hidden width 2560. It is a low-resource dense-model precedent, not a large-MoE
or Agents-A1 result.

The paper reports three independently fitted Jacobian lenses and a set of
lexically adversarial and counterfactual materials-science tasks. Its findings
require a narrower claim boundary than “the mechanism is present in the
representation”:

1. Controlled vocabulary readout was selective, but the broad family-level
   primary comparison did not establish a universal Jacobian advantage over
   direct readout.
2. The three fitted lenses were highly reproducible, but reproducibility did not
   establish that the recovered geometry was mechanistically valid.
3. Target-free vocabulary recovery was not Jacobian-specific; direct readout and
   prompt-surface baselines remained competitive.
4. Absolute hidden-state neighborhoods and graph reconstructions were confounded
   by numerical comparison and failed the paper's broader identifiability tests.
5. Matched raw-state transformations recovered directional-law orientation in
   39 of 40 reported comparisons, but this effect was not Jacobian-specific.
6. Prospective steering succeeded in one narrow matched format while broader
   transfer and patching claims remained limited or negative.

The defensible conclusion is not that absolute hidden-state geometry uniquely
encodes physical mechanisms. The result instead supports a narrower hypothesis:
controlled changes between matched states can reveal relation or policy
orientation that is obscured or confounded in absolute-state geometry.

No attributable immutable companion code repository was located during this
review. All paper results remain external author-supplied evidence until an
independent implementation, artifact, and evaluation audit is completed.

## Binding claim decomposition

Future work must keep these claims separate:

1. **Readability:** a trained or constructed map can recover selected vocabulary
   or labels from a state.
2. **Fit stability:** repeated fits produce similar maps or rankings.
3. **Absolute-state organization:** distances, neighborhoods, graphs, or clusters
   in individual states correspond to a proposed mechanism.
4. **Transition organization:** a controlled difference between two matched
   states corresponds to a relation, requirement, policy, or outcome change.
5. **Mechanism identifiability:** the proposed interpretation survives matched
   semantic, lexical, numerical, positional, and answer-scaffold controls.
6. **Causal influence:** intervening on a direction changes the proposed internal
   or behavioral variable under frozen controls.
7. **Objective-outcome value:** the signal predicts externally established
   correctness, safety, or task success.
8. **Policy utility:** a frozen controller improves outcome and cost without
   violating risk, privacy, or production gates.

Passing one item does not establish any later item. A stable decoder can be a
stable measurement instrument while the scientific interpretation of its output
is wrong. A readable direction can be epiphenomenal. A causal output change can
be incorrect, degenerate, or unsafe.

## Matched counterfactual state-transition gate

Any claim based on a state difference, trajectory difference, route difference,
or Jacobian-transport difference must use prospectively frozen matched
counterfactuals when technically possible.

Freeze at minimum:

- checkpoint, tokenizer or processor, chat template, precision, runtime, layer,
  token boundary, pooling, normalization, and instrumentation path;
- task family, objective outcome rule, evidence source, verifier, and decision
  boundary;
- pair or triplet construction rule and all allowed nuisance differences;
- prompt length, response scaffold, candidate vocabulary, answer order,
  numerical magnitude, sign, unit, position, and formatting controls;
- tool schema, permissions, environment state, prior trajectory, cache state,
  batch state, and serving state for agent tasks;
- state-difference operation, alignment, centering, scaling, sign convention,
  aggregation, threshold, and abstention behavior;
- training, tuning, certification, and sealed-test populations.

At minimum include:

1. a matched pair where the objective relation or correct action changes;
2. a neutral or no-change control;
3. a lexical or numerical inversion where surface direction changes but the
   underlying mechanism does not;
4. a semantic inversion where the mechanism changes while surface form remains
   closely matched;
5. answer-label and candidate-order permutations;
6. family-disjoint and template-disjoint evaluation;
7. correct and incorrect examples matched on surface direction and magnitude.

For agent tasks, matched contrasts should hold the task, tool schema,
environment, and trajectory prefix fixed while changing one prospectively
specified requirement, permission, precondition, evidence item, tool result, or
irreversible-action consequence. Later outcomes, verifier results, or sealed
labels may not enter the feature computation or pair-selection rule.

## Mandatory representation comparators

Every compatible state-transition study must compare, at the identical decision
boundary:

- absolute hidden state;
- matched raw hidden-state difference;
- final logits, entropy, margins, and calibrated confidence;
- ordinary logit-lens or direct-unembedding readout;
- direct-readout difference;
- Jacobian-lens readout;
- Jacobian-lens difference;
- lexical, numerical, positional, task-family, and metadata baselines;
- bounded trajectory summaries;
- external deterministic checks and verifier state;
- router or expert-path differences for admitted MoE models;
- peer-model or dense-sibling representations when available.

A positive matched-state result is not Jacobian-specific by default. A
Jacobian-lens claim requires sealed incremental value over raw-state differences,
direct readout, logit lens, output statistics, metadata, external checks, and
all other cheaper admitted comparators.

## Identifiability and geometry gate

Distances, neighborhoods, graphs, clusters, nearest concepts, and low-dimensional
plots are descriptive objects until their interpretation survives explicit
identifiability tests.

Required controls include:

- label-blind extraction and frozen graph or clustering hyperparameters;
- random, size-matched, degree-matched, and feature-permutation baselines;
- alternate distance metrics, normalizations, layers, seeds, and fit corpora;
- numerical-comparison, lexical-overlap, answer-identity, and prompt-family
  controls;
- arbitrary answer-code and label-name permutations;
- held-out mechanism, task, and model-family evaluation;
- comparison of absolute-state and matched-transition geometry;
- alternative representations satisfying the same behavioral criterion.

High exact or rank reproducibility across fitted lenses establishes instrument
stability only. It does not establish unique mechanism identity, causal
sufficiency, completeness, or objective correctness. Repeated fits on the same
model family and corpus are repeated measurements, not independent task-level or
model-level replicates.

A transition direction may still be a generic detector of change, numerical
ordering, sign, answer scaffold, confidence, or trajectory phase. Neutral
anchors, semantic inversions, counter-numerical controls, and family-held-out
evaluation are required before assigning a mechanism label.

## Jacobian-specificity and estimator gate

All existing exact-estimator, runtime, forward, activation-VJP,
activation-JVP, finite-difference, route, cache, and tensor-consumption gates
remain binding.

In addition:

- source and target states must be aligned under a frozen rule before any
  Jacobian or state difference is computed;
- fit-to-fit agreement must be reported separately from task validity;
- lens fits, fit corpora, seeds, merge rules, and artifact identities must remain
  distinct in the audit record;
- no fitted-lens replicate may be counted as an independent task or model sample;
- direct and raw-state transition baselines must use the same examples, layers,
  positions, and outcome labels;
- a Jacobian advantage selected on one task family must be confirmed on a
  prospectively held-out family without threshold, direction, or layer retuning.

Failure of absolute geometry does not imply absence of readable information.
Success of a matched state difference does not imply a global workspace,
mechanistic uniqueness, objective correctness, or a need for Jacobian transport.

## Intervention separation

Activation addition, subtraction, patching, Jacobian-transpose injection,
forced routing, expert substitution, retry, truncation, and early exit remain
separate interventions.

Any intervention study must freeze:

- direction source, sign, dose, layer, token boundary, duration, and normalization;
- sham, random, direct-readout, raw-difference, opposite-sign, and no-intervention
  controls;
- objective outcome, right-to-wrong, wrong-to-right, degeneration, refusal,
  calibration, latency, and cost metrics;
- transfer tasks, answer vocabularies, paraphrases, and unrelated-capability
  controls;
- fail-closed fallback and irreversible-action boundary.

Selective steering of one relation under one answer format cannot support a
general repair, safety, or control claim. Passive detection must pass its sealed
gate before intervention begins.

## Established

- Stable Jacobian fits can coexist with weak or confounded mechanism geometry.
- Absolute-state geometry and matched state-transition geometry are distinct
  scientific objects.
- Matched counterfactual differences are a mandatory future comparator when the
  task permits controlled pairs.
- A matched state-difference effect is not Jacobian-specific unless it beats raw
  and direct-readout differences under sealed evaluation.
- Fit reproducibility, mechanism identifiability, causal influence, objective
  correctness, and control utility are separate endpoints.
- Existing privacy, sealed-data, verifier, provenance, derivative, intervention,
  and production gates remain intact.

## Unproven

- Independent reproduction of arXiv `2607.20058v1`.
- Unique physical-mechanism geometry in the reported model.
- A universal Jacobian advantage over direct or raw-state readout.
- Transfer from materials-science tasks to coding, tool use, long-horizon agents,
  MoEs, or either Agents-A1 checkpoint.
- Objective-error prediction from matched state changes.
- Incremental router or Jacobian-Lens value over every cheaper comparator.
- Complete Q35Q strict loading, forward parity, or derivative admission.
- Safe early exit, retry, repair, truncation, route intervention, activation
  steering, or production deployment.

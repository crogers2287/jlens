## Binding role of the 4B artifact

Because the 4B checkpoint is dense, it is not an architecture-matched small MoE
for Agents-A1 35B. Even after full artifact admission and only after the M39 gate,
it may be used solely for separately preregistered generic engineering questions,
including:

1. exact-autograd versus reduced-target VJP implementation checks;
2. bounded finite-difference approximation checks;
3. hidden-state capture and phase-localized parity mechanics;
4. deterministic tokenization, load, finite-value, memory, and runtime fixtures;
5. generic Jacobian rank-stability and approximation-error measurements.

It may not validate or support claims about:

- router telemetry or router-control subspaces;
- routed or shared expert contribution;
- expert semantics, expert paths, dispatch, or load balancing;
- MoE-specific Jacobian structure;
- transfer to Agents-A1 35B;
- safe truncation, early exit, intervention, steering, or production behavior.

No 4B result may be described as an Agents-A1 35B result. No cross-model pooling
may satisfy power, class-balance, calibration, minimum-effect, approximation,
replication, or production gates.

## Split surrogate requirement before the 35B backward smoke

The former phrase “smaller-model approximation gate” is now interpreted as two
independent, separately preregistered engineering gates:

### Gate A — admitted Agents-A1 4B dense tooling gate

This gate tests generic Jacobian/VJP/finite-difference correctness and resource
behavior only. It requires the complete existing artifact-admission audit,
immutable revision and file identities, exact configuration inspection,
output-disabled fixtures, phase-localized parity, finite-value checks, memory and
runtime bounds, and privacy-safe cleanup.

### Gate B — separately admitted open-MoE instrumentation gate

A distinct small or medium open MoE must be selected prospectively and admitted
under an equally strict immutable-artifact record. The study must freeze exact
router, expert, dispatch, shared-expert, residual-combination, and gradient-target
definitions before outcome-bearing capture. It must test router/expert telemetry,
contribution formulas, reduced-target gradients, finite differences, parity,
approximation error, rank stability, memory, runtime, and privacy without claiming
transfer to Agents-A1 35B.

A candidate may be considered only because its primary artifacts expose weights,
configuration, code, router/expert tensors, and a supported autograd path. Fully
open models such as OLMoE-1B-7B are leads, not selected or admitted artifacts.
Model choice, revision, task population, features, targets, endpoints, thresholds,
power, multiplicity, and decision rules require a later preregistration.

Neither gate substitutes for the other. Passing the dense gate does not validate
MoE instrumentation. Passing a different-MoE gate does not establish Agents-A1
transfer. If either required gate is blocked or fails, the 35B backward smoke
remains blocked.
